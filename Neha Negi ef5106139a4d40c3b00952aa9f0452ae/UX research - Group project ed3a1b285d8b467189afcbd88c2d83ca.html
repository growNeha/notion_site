UX research - Group project

When:

I took a course in ‘Design Thinking’ in the last semester of my masters () and we were assigned in groups and our task was to conduct user research across IIT-Delhi campus for the given problem.

Problem : We need to increase student engagement during classroom sessions. Find out what are the design opportunities to enhance the classroom furniture to maximise student engagement

Team:

We were a team of 5 students and we used to discuss our action plan in in-person meetings. We divided work amongst us in those meetings and showed up in next meeting(discussed on Whatsapp on when to have) with respective work. I and Mohit took user interviews, while Richa and Devansh took user surveys. Abinash took to the FGD discussions, with me as helper for taking notes and recording the session.

We took 2 weeks time to come up with the final report.

Research report :

Learnings apart from report results :

  • We learned how to operate in a team for a project like this. It needs high collaboration at the beginning because everyone needs to agree on a action plan. Since we did not have an assigned leader, we had to overcome friction through talking and making sure we are moving forward while everyone’s opinion is heard. Mohit took the role of a leader over discussions eventually which helped us reduce the friction we were having at start
  • We got to know different segments of students have different problems. Bachelor students have a large class strength ~ 120 students, while Masters and Design students have a smaller class strength of ~30 students. So we decided to focus on just smaller class strength segment.
  • Conducting user research with different stakeholders helped us look at the problem from different angles
  • I had conducted user interviews and surveys before, but I got to be a part of FGD for the first time and learnt how to conduct one through my peer
